INTERIOR AID

The art and science of upgrading a building's interior to provide a healthier and more aesthetically pleasant atmosphere for those using the area is known as interior design. A person who designs, investigates, coordinates, and supervises such improvement projects is known as an interior designer. In addition to conceptual creation, space planning, site inspections, programming, research, interacting with project stakeholders, construction management, and design execution, interior design is a diverse career.

Interior design used to be an innate element of the construction process. The growth of society and the intricate architecture that came about as a result of the development of industrial processes are both factors that contributed to the profession of interior design.

The goal of efficient space utilization, user comfort, and practical design has aided in the growth of the modern interior design profession. The phrase "interior decorator," which is frequently used in the US, is not synonymous with the profession of interior design. In the UK, where the practice of interior design is still not properly regulated, it is not yet recognized as a profession.
